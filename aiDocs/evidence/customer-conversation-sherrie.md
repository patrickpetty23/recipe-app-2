# Customer Conversation — Sherrie

**Date:** April 2026
**App version:** Earlier version (before Walmart integration and major UI improvements)
**Relationship to team:** Not a friend or family member

## Profile

- Experienced home cook, cooks from recipes 3-4 times per week
- Uses Recipe Keeper app to store and organize recipes
- Prefers in-store shopping — wants to see and select her own produce, find deals in person
- Shops at Smith's, Walmart, Sam's Club — not loyal to one store
- Has used Lose It app for calorie tracking

## Pre-App Pain Points

- Recipe organization is her biggest frustration — wants her app to auto-categorize recipes
- Recipe Keeper doesn't transfer calorie data well to Lose It
- Calorie calculation from recipes is manual and tedious — had to calculate granola recipe ingredient by ingredient

## App Walkthrough Observations

- Imported a recipe via URL (link was pre-saved for her)
- Appreciated that it pulled ingredients automatically: "I love that mine does that... support it so much easier"
- Liked that it provided instructions alongside ingredients
- Immediately asked about calorie information — "What I'd really like is calories. When I'm counting."
- Explored shopping list features

## Walmart/Cart Reaction

- **Does not want cart integration.** Prefers in-store shopping where she can pick her own produce, find deals, and browse.
- "I just want a list. 'Cause I'm gonna run around."
- "It doesn't matter... I'm gonna buy it anyway if I'm gonna make a recipe" — price display isn't valuable to her
- Wanted the list exported to Apple Notes or Apple Lists instead: "That would be so cool if it was on my notes?"

## Key Feedback

1. **Export to Notes/Lists:** Strongest feature request. Wants to check items off in her preferred app, not inside the recipe app.
2. **Calorie counting integration:** Wants the app to calculate calories per serving from the ingredient list. Her current workflow (Recipe Keeper → Lose It) is broken — calorie transfer is inaccurate.
3. **Recipe organization:** Wants automatic categorization of saved recipes.
4. **Calendar integration:** Would like meal plans to sync to Google Calendar so she doesn't forget to check the app.
5. **Different user type:** She represents in-store shoppers who value control and deal-hunting over convenience automation.

## Would You Use This Regularly?

Implied yes for the recipe import + list generation. Would NOT use the Walmart cart integration.

## Key Quotes

- "I just want a list. 'Cause I'm gonna run around."
- "That would be so cool if it was on my notes?"
- "I want to see what's fair... I'll be there and I'll pick up, like, for Smith's, there were pineapples for 99¢. I would not have added that."
- "I won't buy grapes unless they're super good."

## Product Implications

- **Not all users want cart integration.** Sherrie represents a meaningful segment: experienced cooks who shop in-person and value control over automation. For this segment, the app's value is purely in the recipe→list conversion, not the list→cart step.
- **Export is the bridge.** If the app can't export to Notes/Lists, it's asking users like Sherrie to maintain two apps — one for recipes and one for their actual shopping list. That's a dealbreaker for adoption.
- **Calorie counting is a real gap.** Multiple apps (Recipe Keeper, Lose It) fail at accurate calorie calculation from recipe ingredients. If this app can do it well, that's a differentiator independent of Walmart integration.
- **This interview was conducted on the earlier version of the app** before major UI improvements and Walmart integration were added. Sherrie's feedback reflects the core recipe-to-list flow.
