# Customer Conversation — Jane

**Date:** April 2026
**App version:** Current (Expo/React Native with Walmart integration, Planner tab)
**Relationship to team:** Family member (sister-in-law), but represents the exact target demographic

## Profile

- Young mom with small children
- Grocery shops on Mondays — orders Sunday night via Walmart+ and Sam's Club curbside
- Mostly orders the same items weekly, fills in gaps when making a recipe
- Cooks from recipes roughly once a week or every other week
- Stores recipes in a photo album on her phone (screenshots)
- Spontaneous recipe trips go to Target (2-hour pickup)
- Wishes Walmart would auto-populate her usual order instead of just showing past purchases
- Has used Recipe Keeper (mom's app) — finds it not user-friendly

## Pre-App Pain Points

- **Decision fatigue:** "The brain power to just decide what to make for the week is the worst part." Multiple friends share this frustration.
- **Recipe organization:** Has recipes scattered in a phone photo album — "it's actually pretty annoying to scroll through"
- **Wants her usual order auto-populated** — current Walmart reorder shows all past items but doesn't pre-build a cart

## App Walkthrough Observations

### First Impressions
- Saw error log on initial load (the crypto bug — noted but dismissed)
- Found the personalization/preferences screen for the AI planner
- Navigated to recipes tab — saw pre-loaded demo recipes
- Tried to create a collection (dinner) — wasn't immediately clear how to add recipes to it

### Shopping List
- Added chicken masala ingredients to shopping list
- Understood the select-ingredients flow
- Used "Find on Walmart" — appreciated seeing prices without leaving the app
- **Key question:** "Do you just have Walmart?" — wants Sam's Club and other stores

### Walmart Reaction
- **Positive and practical.** Liked seeing prices inline. Immediately asked about Sam's Club integration.
- When told multi-store was planned: "So it's like I could be on here, like I have my go-to's" — sees it as her weekly planning hub.

### Recipe Library
- Liked that recipes have AI-generated photos
- "I literally just have a photo album on my phone... if I could just be like, what do I want for dinner this week? I would use it."

### Cooking Mode
- Looked at the steps tab — "I liked that" (had seen it in a previous brief demo)

### Nutrition Tracker
- First reaction: "Can you track your weight on here?" — then reconsidered: "I kind of like that it's not about weight loss, that it's just purely nutrition."
- Wanted the AI to help figure out nutrition goals — not obvious that the chat could do this

### Chat/Import
- **Did not find the URL import easily.** The chat interface didn't make it obvious that you can paste URLs.
- "It doesn't say anything about adding recipes from the Internet" — the welcome suggestions don't mention URL import
- Suggested adding "Add a URL from your favorite recipe website" to the chat welcome message
- Prefers to Google a recipe, find one with good ratings, and paste the URL — doesn't want AI to pick for her

## Key Feedback

1. **Multi-store support (Sam's Club):** Her #1 request. Shops at both Walmart and Sam's Club — wants price comparison without leaving the app.
2. **Recipe sharing / social collections:** "That would be a game changer." Wants to add friends, share recipe collections (like Goodreads for recipes). All her mom friends would share recipes. This was her most enthusiastic feature request.
3. **URL import discoverability:** The chat interface hides the URL import capability. Needs a clear prompt or button for importing from websites.
4. **Product alternatives in Walmart:** When eggs showed as 6-count, she wanted to see other options (18-count, different brands). "I'm picky about my brand of sourdough."
5. **AI-powered brand preferences:** After switching from Great Value to a preferred brand once, the app should remember and default to it next time.
6. **Onboarding tips per tab:** Suggested tooltip popups when visiting a new tab for the first time (e.g., "The AI can help you figure out your nutrition goals").
7. **Decision fatigue is the real problem:** "The brain power to just decide what to make for the week is the worst part." The recipe library and planner directly address this.

## Would You Use This Regularly?

"Yeah. For sure... I would use it." Most excited about recipe organization, meal planning, and the social/sharing concept.

## Product Implications

- **Social recipe sharing is a potential growth engine.** Jane was more excited about sharing collections with friends than any other feature. This creates a natural viral loop — each user who shares brings in their cooking friends. No competitor tested (Paprika, Yummly, AnyList) offers friend-to-friend collection sharing.
- **URL import needs to be more discoverable in the chat interface.** Two users now (Jane and the original UX finding) have missed this capability. The chat welcome message should explicitly mention it.
- **Multi-store is validated by a third user** (after Thomas and Kierra). Jane shops at both Walmart and Sam's Club — she wants comparison, not just one store.
- **Decision fatigue, not recipe extraction, is the core pain for weekly planners.** Jane doesn't struggle with typing ingredients — she struggles with *deciding what to cook*. The Planner tab and recipe library address this directly. This reframes the value proposition for her segment.
- **Brand preferences and product alternatives** extend the Walmart integration into a more personalized shopping experience. This was also mentioned by Kierra (international stores) and is a consistent pattern.
